I finally did it! <Emoji type="party" /> After  focusing almost exclusively on programming websites for the last 5 years, I started my personal curriculum on learning Blender! 

As much as I love coding mini project + doing work tasks, I’ve felt my digital art skills slipping away, and that creative itch has become impossible to ignore lately as I contemplate where I am in life as I turn 24... <Emoji type="embarrassed" />

Instead of just going back to 2D drawing, I wanted something new, something challenging—something that could bring the worlds I’ve had locked in my head to life! I’ve spent so much time creating <MyLink src="https://pin.it/8Yczbozb8">Pinterest boards</MyLink> filled with 3D character models and scenery; it was time to stop lurking and start building myself. <Emoji type="smile" />

I truly believe that if I put my heart into this, I can achieve something massive.

My ultimate vision? <Emoji type="sarcastic" /> That 90s anime aesthetic and low poly stuff. I want to create those atmospheric animations I see on the small YouTube channels I follow. I want to build my own universe and finally bring the story of the Celestial Exiles and my own characters into a 3D reality!

---

Yesterday was about laying the foundation. I had a specific checklist to get me off the ground:

- **The Plan:** Install the latest Blender, learn viewport navigation (pan, zoom, orbit), and understand the divide between Object and Edit Mode.
- **The Reality:** I installed the software and even added a custom purple-pink theme~! <Emoji type="smilewide" />
- **The Learning:** I followed a tutorial to get comfortable with the "Big Three": Rotate, Move (Pan), and Scale. I also learned the shortcut to add new mesh objects!
- **The Discovery:** Even though the video didn't cover it, it was still on my to-do list required for yesterday and today; I discovered the TAB key to switch between Object and Edit Mode. I’ll admit, I rushed it a bit since it was late at night, but today I will study it better! <Emoji type="heart" />
