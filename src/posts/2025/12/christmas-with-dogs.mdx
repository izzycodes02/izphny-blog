# Keeping Your Dog Safe During Christmas

![Dog in Christmas sweater](/images/dog-christmas.jpg)

The holiday season is magical, but it comes with unique dangers for our four-legged friends. Here's how to keep your dog safe while still enjoying all the festivities.

## Christmas Tree Safety

That beautiful tree is like a giant, interesting toy for your dog. Be careful with:

- **Tree water** – Can contain fertilizers or bacteria
- **Ornaments** – Broken glass and choking hazards
- **Lights** – Chewing cords can cause electric shocks
- **Tinsel** – Especially dangerous if swallowed (can cause intestinal blockages)

> "The best Christmas gift? A healthy, happy dog by the fire." – Unknown

## Dangerous Foods

![Dog eyeing Christmas dinner](/images/dog-christmas-dinner.jpg)

Keep these away from curious noses:

❌ **Chocolate** – Toxic to dogs (especially dark chocolate)
❌ **Grapes and raisins** – Can cause kidney failure
❌ **Onions and garlic** – Damages red blood cells
❌ **Xylitol** – Found in sugar-free treats and gum
❌ **Cooked bones** – Can splinter and cause internal damage

## Safe Treats

Instead, you can give:

- Plain cooked turkey (no skin, no bones)
- Carrot sticks
- Green beans
- Special dog-safe Christmas treats from pet stores

## Gift Giving for Dogs

![Dog with wrapped present](/images/dog-present.jpg)

If you're buying gifts for your pup:

- Choose appropriate size toys (nothing they can swallow)
- Avoid toys with small parts that can be chewed off
- Consider puzzle toys for mental stimulation
- New beds are always appreciated

## Creating a Calm Environment

The holidays can be overwhelming with visitors and noise:

- Create a quiet space your dog can retreat to
- Let guests know not to bother a sleeping or hiding dog
- Maintain regular walk and feeding schedules
- Consider calming aids if your dog is anxious

## Emergency Prep

Know your vet's emergency contact information and have a 24-hour emergency vet number handy. Better safe than sorry!
